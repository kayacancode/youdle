Shining a Light on Emerging Food Safety Threats

MEMPHIS, Tenn. (Youdle) – An alarming increase in foodborne illnesses has put a spotlight on the importance of food safety. Recent data indicates that the threat posed by unsafe foods is global and calls for unified action across the entire food supply chain.

Worrisome findings include a genetic cluster of Extended-Spectrum β-lactamase–Producing Klebsiella pneumoniae in humans and food in Switzerland, a retrospective analysis of historical Listeria monocytogenes clinical isolates in New York, USA, and the emergence of multidrug-resistant Salmonella enterica in poultry and humans in Israel.

  • Escherichia coli O157:H7 associated with multiple food sources in the United States
  • Community outbreak of OXA-48–Producing Escherichia coli linked to food premises in New Zealand
  • Effects of decentralized sequencing on national Listeria monocytogenes genomic surveillance in Australia

These findings underscore the need for vigilance when purchasing and preparing food. Stay informed about food safety threats through platforms like Youdle, and always practice safe food handling and storage.
